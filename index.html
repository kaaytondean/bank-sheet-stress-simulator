Bank Stress Simulator (SVB-Inspired)

Adjust the balance-sheet and run dynamics below to see how fast risk can compound in a high-rate environment.

About the Author

My name is Kayton Dean I am an undergraduate student focused on computer science and system-level modeling. This simulator was built to translate my academic research during my senior year of high school on the Silicon Valley Bank collapse into a computational prototype, exploring how interest-rate shocks, deposit composition, and withdrawal speed can interact to produce liquidity stress.

This project reflects my interest in applied computer science and engineering—moving beyond theoretical analysis to build interactive systems that model real-world financial risk.

Inputs

Rate hikes increase unrealized losses on longer-duration securities.

Higher uninsured deposits can accelerate withdrawals under stress.

Higher duration means bigger price sensitivity to rate changes.

Large unrealized losses reduce confidence and constrain liquidity options.

Digital banking + concentrated networks can make runs extremely fast.

More concentrated depositors can move together and amplify flight risk.
